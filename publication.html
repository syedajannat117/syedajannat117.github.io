Recommendation System: History, Present and Future

Jannatul Ferdaus

Arxiv

Abstract Bibtext PDF
Recommendation systems in online platforms, such as product purchasing, service consumption, and social media (e.g., Facebook, Twitter, Reddit, WeChat, LinkedIn), have significantly enhanced the user experience and engagement. These systems have been evolving to reduce search time and increase time spent on platforms, driven by the vast amounts of data available. Many studies on recommendation systems focus on users' history, search behavior, and interaction with items or products. This study provides an overview of the evolution of recommendation systems, from traditional methods like collaborative filtering to content-based filtering and hybrid approaches. It analyzes how recommendation systems work, their development, and the key factors influencing their effectiveness. The paper examines the historical context of recommendation systems, current advancements, and the drivers for re-evaluating these systems in terms of efficiency and platform utilization. It highlights the increasing interest in leveraging Large Language Models (LLMs) such as BERT, GPT, LLaMA, Copilot, and Gemini for enhanced recommendation capabilities. Looking ahead, recommendation systems are expected to become more personalized and adaptive with the integration of advanced technologies like LLMs and novel methodologies such as prompt engineering and reinforcement learning.
